# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary user is a Chinese graduate student or early-stage researcher who follows embodied intelligence and large language model research continuously. They work primarily on a desktop, often in long reading sessions, and need to move quickly from discovery to filtering, explanation, saving, and later review.

## Product Purpose

Paperfield collects recent research and public GitHub projects, selects a small high-quality weekly paper list and a compact daily project list, resolves legal open-access PDFs, and turns papers and source code into multilingual reading workflows. Success means the user can identify the few papers and projects worth studying next, inspect source evidence, understand methods and implementations, ask grounded questions, and preserve a useful research trail without maintaining spreadsheets or browser tabs.

## Brand Personality

Calm, exacting, research-minded. It should feel like a trusted lab instrument with editorial judgment, not a social feed, marketing site, or noisy analytics dashboard.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI dashboards with purple gradients, glowing cards, and inflated metrics.
- Endless equal-sized card grids that make every paper look equally important.
- News feeds optimized for engagement rather than careful reading.
- Academic databases that expose every metadata field before helping the reader decide.
- Dark terminal themes used only to signal technical sophistication.

## Design Principles

1. Make the next reading decision obvious.
2. Preserve scholarly evidence and clearly label generated interpretation.
3. Use density for comparison, not decoration.
4. Let filters stay visible and predictable during long research sessions.
5. Treat reading state, favorites, and explanations as one continuous workflow.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, full keyboard focus visibility, semantic controls, Chinese-first copy, reduced-motion support, responsive layouts, and no color-only status communication.
